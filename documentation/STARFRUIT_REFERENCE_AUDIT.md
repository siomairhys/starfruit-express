# Starfruit Express — Reference Audit

> Internal audit completed for the Starfruit Express redesign concept.
> Live inspection was performed via fetched markdown + hero screenshot
> of the official website (https://www.starfruitexpress.com/, /about,
> /contact). Visual references were captured at desktop width; mobile
> inspection was performed against the same source markup.

## Existing brand characteristics

- **Personality:** warm, human, operator-first. Tone leans on phrases
  like *"The Restaurant's Ally"* and *"We only get paid when you profit"* —
  approachable but operational.
- **Voice:** plainspoken, empathetic, restaurant-floor-aware
  (*"Calling vendors, chasing deliveries, fixing shortages…"*).
- **Audience:** independent and small-group restaurant operators,
  primarily in Florida.

## Existing logo treatment

- Star-shaped citrus mark (a stylized starfruit) paired with a custom
  "Starfruit Express" wordmark and a small "Food Procurement & Logistics"
  qualifier.
- Mark uses gradient yellow → orange → green. Wordmark uses a clean
  geometric sans, with "Express" set in a contrasting weight.

## Existing color palette

- **Forest deep** — used for headings, buttons, and the top nav background.
- **Warm cream** — primary editorial background.
- **Citrus orange / gold** — accent for emphasis and CTAs.
- **Fresh green** — for trust/promise badges ("Our Promise").
- Subtle warm grain texture in section backgrounds.

## Existing typography direction

- A bold, slightly condensed display sans for the editorial headlines
  (with selective italic for emphasis).
- A neutral humanist sans for body copy.
- Small mono / uppercase labels for eyebrows ("Time is Money. We Save You Both.").

## Existing imagery style

- Warm, golden-hour restaurant photography (modern dining room with
  cinematic landscape behind it).
- Documentary kitchen photography (chaotic kitchen, shaking hands).
- Light grain texture across editorial backgrounds.

## Tone of voice

- Empathetic and operational; never gimmicky.
- Frames problems as *theirs first*: "It became a daily battle just to
  stay ahead."

## Primary audience

- Restaurant operators in Florida.
- 1–10 locations.
- Operators who balance front-of-house, suppliers, deliveries, and finance themselves.

## Current services (as messaged on the live site)

- Purchasing / procurement support.
- Supplier visibility and rebate / credit recovery.
- Temperature-controlled refrigerated delivery ("Specialized vehicles
  maintain ideal temperatures for all your food products").
- Operational support ("Manage orders, handle claims, track your
  orders – all in one place").
- Fast operations response.

## Existing calls to action

- "Talk with our team" → `/contact`
- "See how we help" → `/solutions`
- "Free consultation · Contact Us" (top-right CTA)
- Phone: (786) 305-7470
- Email: fresh@starfruitexpress.com

## Strong visual elements worth preserving

- The citrus + forest brand palette.
- The starfruit mark.
- The big editorial headline treatment with selective italic emphasis.
- The "Our Promise" floating card pattern.
- The grain texture on editorial backgrounds.

## Opportunities identified (respectful framing)

- *Opportunity to strengthen hierarchy* — the homepage hero headline
  is very long; the dual purchasing + fulfillment value can come
  through faster.
- *Opportunity to improve visual storytelling* — the cold-chain promise
  currently lives as a short bullet on /contact; it deserves a felt
  experience.
- *Opportunity to surface operational value* — purchasing benefits are
  described in prose; a glimpse of the actual product would communicate
  faster.
- *Opportunity to simplify the decision path* — the conversion flow is a
  generic contact form; framing it as an Assessment matches the offer.
- *Opportunity to improve responsive presentation* — long stacked text
  blocks on mobile can be replaced with structured cards.

## Five sections selected for redesign

| # | Section | Source URL |
|---|---------|------------|
| 1 | Homepage hero | https://www.starfruitexpress.com/ |
| 2 | Operational friction (Traditional vs. Starfruit) | https://www.starfruitexpress.com/ |
| 3 | Purchasing intelligence / "Solutions" messaging | https://www.starfruitexpress.com/solutions |
| 4 | Cold-chain fulfillment (Why Choose Us) | https://www.starfruitexpress.com/contact |
| 5 | Contact / conversion → Savings Assessment | https://www.starfruitexpress.com/contact |

### Why each section was selected

1. **Hero** — the most important moment to communicate the dual identity
   (purchasing + fulfillment) and the operator-first tone.
2. **Operational friction** — a strong existing narrative; benefits
   from a clearer side-by-side workflow visualization.
3. **Purchasing intelligence** — the company's biggest business
   differentiator; deserves a real product surface, not paragraph copy.
4. **Cold-chain fulfillment** — currently understated; visualizing the
   delivery journey makes the temperature-controlled promise tangible.
5. **Contact** — re-framing the contact form as an Assessment matches
   the existing offer ("we'd rather be useful first") and qualifies leads.
